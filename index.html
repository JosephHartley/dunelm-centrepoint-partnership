Dunelm x Centrepoint
Joy of Home Partnership

It takes more than a roof to end youth homelessness. Young people need the joy of home to truly break the cycle of homelessness.

To launch our new 'Joy of Home' charity partnership, Dunelm and Centrepoint commissioned a study asking homeless and vulnerable 16-25 year olds about their experiences of home. 72% of young people associated home with traumatic and negative events that contributed to them becoming homeless. 30% had never experienced a truly happy home. Home is more than just a roof, it's the place from which the rest of our lives can thrive.

This is why Dunelm has chosen Centrepoint to be our new national charity partner. Together, we will put the joy of home at the heart of ending youth homelessness.

We are going to show how a 'home not a hostel' helps young people better recover from the trauma of homelessness. We will help young people ready to live independently get set up in their new home. Crucially, we will raise awareness of 'Stepping Stone Homes', specialist housing for formally homeless young people, driving change and enabling more Stepping Stone Homes to be built across the UK.

"Centrepoint and Dunelm’s ‘Joy of Home’ partnership will transform the lives of young people experiencing homelessness today and end youth homelessness for the next generation. For a young person who has experienced considerable trauma, they need a home, somewhere they can settle and feel safe to learn that they have potential and are deserving of a happy life. It takes a home, not just a roof to truly break the cycle of homelessness and I am delighted that we will be able to bring this to life through our new transformational partnership with Dunelm."

Seyi Obakin, Centrepoint CEO

"Dunelm is in an incredible position, our organisation is able to use the joy of home to help end youth homelessness, it’s an extraordinary privilege to be able to have such a transformational impact on the lives of vulnerable young people. We are hugely excited for our partnership with Centrepoint and looking forward to sharing the impact we have had on this critical issue in the UK today."

Nick Wilkinson, Dunelm CEO
